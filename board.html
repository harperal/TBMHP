Meet Our Amazing Team

Our team is a mix of Tom’s family and friends that are committed to the success of TBMHP and the mission that we strive to obtain.

Lori Batterman


President


Lori currently lives in Elkton and was married to Tom for 22 years. She is a mortgage advisor and is currently taking a leave to concentrate on the success of the Thomas Batterman Mental Health Project. She is also active in the community as a volunteer firefighter and works part-time at the local cafe. After watching Tom struggle for years with depression and substance abuse, she has made it her mission to bring some good out of Tom’s death.

Jessic Batterman


Secretary


Jessica Batterman, Tom’s daughter, is a recent graduate of the University of Oregon with a Bachelor of Science in Physics and a minor in Climate Studies. Following her graduation, Jessica is working as a teaching assistant at the university. Jessica became passionate about improving access to mental health services and researching new therapies after witnessing her father’s struggles with depression and alcoholism. She is thrilled to have the opportunity to help individuals get the help they need through the Thomas Batterman Mental Health Project. Jessica currently lives in Eugene, Oregon with her cat Chase. She enjoys live music, baking, and many outdoor pursuits such as hiking, climbing, and trail running.

Hillel Hoffman


Board Member


After working for more than three decades in journalism and higher education, Hillel opened up an independent communications consultancy and college admissions counseling business in 2020. Originally from Ithaca, New York, where he was a high school friend of Tom Batterman, he now lives in Philadelphia with his wife, Sharon.

Richard Kiely


Board Member


Richard Kiely is the associate vice provost for engagement and land-grant affairs and the director of academic initiatives in the David M. Einhorn Center for Community Engagement at Cornell University. Richard was born and raised in Ithaca, NY. He is married to Andrea and they have two daughters, Eva (18) and Ana (13). Along with his passion for community engagement, he loves hiking in gorges and playing ice hockey. He was one of Tom’s best friends in high school.

Sandra Galli


Board Member


Sandra Galli lives in Elkton, Oregon. Elkton is a small, historic town, overlooking the beautiful Umpqua River and nestled in Oregon’s coastal mountains. Here Sandra enjoys a simple life where she is a city council member, works at a vineyard in the tasting room, is a for hire as a professional gardener, and a first-year beekeeper. Sandra walks regularly along the side of the Umpqua River with a group of ladies and her German Shepherd. This is where she met Lori Batterman.

Sandra has never met Tom Batterman, but has heard his life story through Lori Batterman. Lori found Tom to be “very intelligent, honest, athletic, and unique.” When Lori asked Sandra to join the Tom Batterman Mental Health Project, she accepted the position immediately. When Sandra was born, her father suffered a nervous breakdown and later was diagnosed with manic depressive disorder. She knows first-hand how debilitating mental health illnesses are. Sandra is committed to supporting the TBMHP.
